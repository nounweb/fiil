## Preface: Purpose of the Methodology and Principles of Compression

### 1. Purpose

This methodology aims to reconstruct the logical reasoning structure of courts based on the Constitution and statutes. The Constitution and statutes are primary materials, while precedents are used as argumentative resources that concretize the meaning of those laws. It does not imitate the conclusions of specific precedents or treat them as correct answers; rather, it extracts the argumentative structure of precedents and applies it to the current case. The degree of consistency with actual judgments is a verification indicator of the methodology's validity, not the goal of the methodology itself.

Universal and abstract principles not dependent on specific statutes or precedents must be established as axioms, and from these, specific procedures and standards must be re-derivable.

### 2. Three Principles of Compression

(i) Generality: Axioms are not bound by case types.
(ii) Completeness: The axiom system encompasses all judgment situations.
(iii) Conciseness: Identical normative content exists only in a single axiom.

### 3. Axiom Hierarchy Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      Tier 1: Principle Axioms (A~G)     │  Immutable. Takes precedence over all axioms and procedures.
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│    Tier 1.5: Meaning Interpretation Axioms (S) │  Takes precedence over all interpretation axioms (H~L).
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│      Tier 2: Interpretation Axioms (H~L)│  Subordinate to principle and meaning interpretation axioms.
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│      Tier 3: Enforcement Rules (M, O)   │  Subordinate to the three upper tiers.
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

In case of conflict between tiers, the higher-tier axiom prevails. For conflict criteria within the same tier, refer to the axiom system header.

### 4. Document Drafting Principles (New in v13.0)

These principles are editorial standards to prevent redundancy within the axiom system and maintain the Single Source of Truth.

**(i) Principle for Drafting Axiom Text**
Axioms declare only principles and standards. Procedural execution order, quantitative criteria, and output formats are not described in the axiom text; they are described only in Part 2 STEP and Appendix A, respectively. If the same normative content exists in both an axiom and a STEP, the description in the STEP is removed and replaced with a reference: "→ Apply Axiom [Symbol]."

**(ii) Standard Structure for Mandatory Rules**
All mandatory rules are described using only the following three-line structure.
```
Trigger Condition: (In which cases is this applied)
Consequence of Violation: (Invalid / Deduction of X points / Procedural defect)
Basis Axiom: → Axiom [Symbol]
```
Procedural content is transferred to the relevant axiom, and output format is transferred to Appendix A.

**(iii) Reference Principle**
If the same content exists in two locations, the normatively superior or more general location is designated as the single source. The subordinate or more specific location is replaced with "→ Refer to [Single Source]."

**(iv) Role of Appendix A**
Appendix A is the single source for all output formats. If a block-type output format is required in the axiom text, mandatory rules, or STEP, it is referenced as "→ Appendix A Format [Number]."

**Axiom A [Complete Extraction of Dispute Propositions]**

All judgments begin with the complete extraction of a pair of incompatible propositions (P, Q) between the parties. Omission of a proposition is an omission in the judgment.

**1. Definition of Propositions**
(P) Plaintiff's (or Prosecutor's) claim: "A is B."
(Q) Defendant's (or Suspect's) rebuttal: "A is not B."
P and Q must be such that they cannot both be true, or the legal conclusion requires a binary choice.

**2. Three Layers of Propositions**

**Layer 1 (Propositions on Procedural Requirements):** Jurisdiction, exclusion period, statute of limitations, standing, interest in the lawsuit, etc. These are examined before addressing the merits.

**Layer 2 (Propositions Determining Legal Character):** Propositions concerning the legal character of the claim, applicable statutes, normative evaluation of the act, etc., i.e., which legal framework governs the case.

**Layer 3 (Merits Propositions):** Substantive issues such as fulfillment of constituent elements, illegality, liability, creation, extinction, or modification of rights, scope of damages, etc.

**3. Detection of Hidden Propositions**
Even if not directly derived from the parties' explicit arguments, propositions that the court must examine *ex officio* (propositions on procedural requirements, legal character, or criminal-specific hidden propositions) must be detected and included.
Detection methods: Check Appendix B checklist, check exceptional legal doctrine triggers, verify un-cited requirements of relevant statutes, compare with the legal doctrine group of precedents in similar cases.

※ Cyclical Refinement: For complex cases where detecting hidden propositions is difficult, first perform the general causal decomposition (1st approximation) of Axiom E to grasp the outline of the core legal relationship. Then, based on the result, allow cyclical refinement to supplement hidden propositions. However, even in this case, the initial extraction of explicit propositions (Steps 1 & 2) must precede this.

**4. Proposition Extraction Procedure: 4 Steps**

[Step 1] Structural decomposition of the case description (performed in STEP 0-9)
[Step 2] Initial construction of opposing propositions (performed in STEP 0-9)
[Step 3] Detection of hidden propositions (after STEP A-2, integrated in STEP A-4)
[Step 4] Completeness verification (final confirmation in STEP A-4) □ Comprehensiveness □ Consistency □ Counterfactual verification □ Omission verification

**5. Output Format:** → Output according to the standard format in Appendix A [A-1].

**6. Reflection in Verdict:** → Apply Axiom M (Mandatory completeness of judgment on all propositions)

**7. Duty to Verify All Requirements Before Complex Requirements (New in v13.10, Generalized in v14.1)**
When a norm lists multiple requirements (e.g., "If A and B"),
the fact that one requirement is satisfied does not exempt the verification of the remaining requirements.
Each requirement must be independently satisfied in its entirety; if any one is not satisfied,
the legal effect does not occur.
This principle applies generally to complex requirement structures, regardless of the type of norm (criminal, civil, administrative, contractual):
- Each requirement is separated into an independent proposition, and its individual satisfaction is judged.
- The logic "satisfaction of a prior requirement = presumption of satisfaction of a subsequent requirement" is not permitted.
- If there is a logical dependency (preliminary relationship) between requirements, the order must be specified, and judgment proceeds in that order.
If not performed: Treated as incomplete proposition extraction → Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)" applies.

**0. Principle of Confirming Identity of the Subject of Judgment (New in v14.7)**

Prior to proposition extraction, as a logical precondition for commencing judgment, the following must be confirmed.

It must be verified that the input factual relationship is logically identical to the case subject to judgment. Identity is determined by the consistency of the following three components:

① **Party Consistency**: Do the input parties (plaintiff, defendant, suspect, etc.) match the case number and case name?
② **Claim Purpose Consistency**: Does the input claim statement and cause of action match the case name and case type?
③ **Legal Relationship Type Consistency**: Does the description of the input factual relationship match the type of legal relationship derived from the claim purpose?

If any inconsistency is detected among the above three elements, proposition extraction shall not commence, and judgment shall be reserved. The reason for the inconsistency shall be stated, and a request for supplementary information shall be made.

※ This principle applies before the commencement of any judgment, regardless of the format of the input data (full text, summary, structured).
※ If the input information is insufficient to confirm identity itself, the reason for the inability to confirm shall be stated, and judgment shall be reserved.

Output format:
```
[STEP-A-§0: Confirmation of Identity of Subject of Judgment]
① Party Consistency: Consistent / Inconsistent / Unverifiable
② Claim Purpose Consistency: Consistent / Inconsistent / Unverifiable
③ Legal Relationship Type Consistency: Consistent / Inconsistent / Unverifiable
Judgment: Identity Confirmed → Proceed with Proposition Extraction / Inconsistency Detected → Reserve Judgment + State Reason
```

If not performed: → Appendix C "-2 points (Legal Doctrine)" + Record procedural defect in STEP 0
If proceeding without declaring judgment reservation after detecting inconsistency: → Verdict invalid

**0-1. Principle of Prior Determination of the Framework of Reference (New in v14.7)**

After completing the confirmation of the identity of the subject of judgment (§0), and prior to proposition extraction, the following must be determined.

The standard of judgment — i.e., the scope of adjudicative authority, the intensity of review, and the choice of applicable legal doctrine — must be deduced from the procedural position in which the subject of judgment is placed. Judgment under a framework of reference arbitrarily set from outside is invalid.

① **Determination of Procedural Position**: In which procedural stage is the subject of judgment placed? (Instance level, case type, type of judgment, etc.)
② **Determination of Scope of Authority**: What is the scope of adjudicative authority permitted at the determined procedural position?
③ **Determination of Intensity of Review**: What is the standard of review applied in that procedure? (Full authority of fact-finding court, limitation of appellate court on questions of law, etc.)

Judgment without a determined framework of reference, or under an incorrectly set framework of reference, constitutes a violation of Axiom A.

※ This principle is linked to 0-1-σ (Instance Confirmation). The instance information confirmed in 0-1-σ is used for the determination of ①②③ in this principle.
※ An error in the framework of reference is a fatal error that contaminates all subsequent judgment stages. If an error is detected, immediately follow the re-determination procedure.

If not performed: → Verdict invalid (Judgment under an undetermined framework of reference has no effect)

**8. Principle of Precedence for Judging the Validity of Juristic Acts (New in v13.13)**
Judgment on the validity (validity, nullity, voidability, rescission) of a juristic act
logically precedes judgment on the legal effects arising from that act (taxation, damages, claim for performance, registration, etc.).
If a juristic act is null and void or has been rescinded, judgment on the legal effects premised on that act is, in principle, unnecessary and is omitted.
However, additional exceptional judgments on the precedence relationship are made in the following cases:
① When the issue concerns legal doctrines protecting third parties based on the appearance of a void act (bona fide acquisition, apparent authority, principle of public confidence).
② When the restitution of unjust enrichment based on a void act is a separate issue.
③ When, in a revocation of a tax disposition based on nullity, the scope of nullity is an independent issue.
If not performed: → Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)" applies.

**9. Duty to Confirm the Final Conclusive Effect of a Legal Doctrine Violation (New in v13.14)**
After a violation of legal doctrine is recognized, it must be secondarily confirmed whether the effect of that violation
substantially affects the final conclusion (amount, existence of obligation, whether the claim is granted, etc.).
Recognition of a legal doctrine violation does not automatically mean a change in the conclusion.

Perform the following two steps in order:
① **Confirm the Scope of the Violation's Effect:**
   Even if the relevant legal doctrine violation is excluded or nullified, does the final conclusion (claim amount, existence of obligation, etc.)
   substantially change?
   → If it does not change: Recognize the legal doctrine violation but dismiss the claim or maintain the current conclusion.
   → If it does change: Proceed to change the conclusion or quash and remand.
② **Distinguish Between Intermediate and Final Effects:**
   "Quashing the lower court's judgment" or "judgment on procedural requirements" is different from the final conclusion (plaintiff wins/loses).
   When choosing the conclusion of quash and remand, the direction of the conclusion after remand must be stated together.

※ Typical error patterns:
   - "Recognition of violation of duty to state reasons → Automatic quash and remand" (Error if the conclusion remains unchanged even after exclusion)
   - "Recognition of defect in procedural requirements → Automatic plaintiff win" (Failure to review final effect)
If not performed: → Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)" applies.
---
**Axiom B [Reverse Verification and Balance]**

Prior to any judgment, the strongest legal doctrinal argument from the defendant’s perspective shall be constructed based on legal principles. Arguments that have been definitively rejected by a higher court are inadmissible.

The plaintiff’s counterarguments shall be constructed with equal volume and depth as the defendant’s arguments and verified equally.

**Mandatory Inclusions (Execution Procedure Including Quantity Standards):** → Refer to STEP A-1

**Principle of Allocation of Burden of Proof:** The party asserting the 'occurrence, existence, or content' of any right, fact, or legal relationship bears the burden of proof thereof. The burden of proving the non-existence itself cannot be imposed on a party seeking legal effects on the grounds of 'non-existence.'

※ **Principle of Shift of Burden of Proof After Proving Requisite Facts (Newly Established in v13.10)**: If the party asserting a right proves the existence of the requisite facts, the opposing party asserting the non-existence or lack of value of a specific element constituting those requisite facts bears the burden of proving it.
   In other words, proving the existence of requisite facts has the effect of shifting the burden of proof regarding the non-existence of that element to the opposing party.
   (Example: A party who proves the existence of a claim is not responsible for proving the lack of value of that claim; the lack of value is proven by the opposing party asserting it.)

**Counterargument:** Before reaching the final conclusion, state the single strongest legal doctrinal argument that could overturn the conclusion, and explain why that argument does not overturn the conclusion. In case of non-compliance → Apply the deduction standards in Appendix C.

※ **Limitations on the Use of Value Hierarchy (Axiom D)**: The first to third priority values of Axiom D serve as directional standards for legal doctrinal judgment and cannot be used as arguments to exclude the conclusive effects of procedural law (such as res judicata, succession effect, or corporate entity independence) during the reverse verification stage.
---
**Axiom C [Substance Over Form, but Strict Compliance with Mandatory Rules and Penalty Provisions]**

The nature of a legal relationship shall be determined not by the name given by the parties, the form of the document, or the wording of the contract, but by the economic substance of the act, the flow of funds, the attribution of risk and liability, and the reasonable expectations of the parties.

However, prior to the substance determination, the following shall be verified in order:

**(1)** First, distinguish whether the act in question is a juristic act or a factual act. Only a juristic act intended to create, modify, or extinguish legal rights and obligations constitutes a regulated "transaction."

**(2) [Distinction between Procedural Rules and Substantive Rights]**
Violation or non-compliance with procedural obligations (registration, reporting, notification, application, etc.) shall, in principle, only be subject to sanctions for such violation and shall not be interpreted as automatically extinguishing or depriving the parties of their substantive rights. For a procedural rule to be interpreted as affecting substantive rights, it must be justified by a comprehensive review of the rule's text, normative purpose, and proportionality. Whether the effect of a procedural violation extends to the extinction of substantive rights shall first be verified under Axiom H, Priority 1.5 (Textual Clarity Gate).

**(3) [Review of the Source Statute of the Obligation]** → Apply Axiom E, Layer ⑤

**(4) [Analysis of Legal Connectivity between Dispositions]** → Apply Axiom E, Layer ⑥

**(5)** Where the requirements of a mandatory rule are prescribed, if those requirements are not met, the validity shall be denied before examining the substance. It shall be expressly stated that no right arises in the event of a violation of a mandatory rule.

※ Regarding mandatory rules and penalty provisions, Axiom H's Priority 1.5 (Textual Clarity Gate) and Priority 1 (Strict Interpretation of Penalty Provisions) take precedence.

※ **Principle of Textual Primacy in Interpreting Dispositive Documents (Newly established in v13.9)**: When interpreting a dispositive document (contract, agreement, settlement, collective agreement, etc.) directly prepared and signed by the parties,
   Axiom H, Priority 1.5 (Textual Clarity Gate) takes precedence over this Axiom (Substance Over Form).
   If the text of the dispositive document is clear, substantive interpretation is permitted only as a supplementary measure.
   Axiom C (Substance Over Form) is triggered only when the text is unclear or when it is proven that the parties' true intent differs significantly from the text.

※ v13.0: The content of the former Axiom T (Resolution of Form-Substance Conflict) is absorbed into this Axiom. Paragraph 2 of Axiom C encompasses T-1 (Principles of Interpretation in Form-Substance Conflict) and T-2 (Principle of Recognizing the Dispositive Nature of Administrative Acts). The expression "Axiom T" is no longer used.
---
**Axiom D [Value Hierarchy]**

Priority regarding the direction of judgment:

First Priority: Individual life, body, and personal legal interests > Financial interests of public institutions.
Second Priority: Protection of innocent victims > Property rights of the perpetrator.
Third Priority: Protection of the vulnerable (workers, consumers, information-disadvantaged persons, etc.) > Freedom of contract.
Fourth Priority: Legal stability > Concrete validity. Exception: Where a violation of constitutional fundamental rights is clear.
Fifth Priority: Procedural requirements prescribed by statutes > Substantive rights. Exception: Where a procedural violation is so serious and manifest as to render it void ab initio.
---
**Axiom E [Causal Decomposition]**

Where multiple causes contribute to a single legal consequence, each cause is evaluated separately according to its nature, degree of contribution, and whether it satisfies its individual requirements.

Legal and factual acts are decomposed into the following layers, and each layer is individually assessed.

① The underlying cause of the occurrence of the claim/obligation
② The attribution of benefits and the purpose of settlement
③ The role of a third party: whether a mere performance assistant or an intermediary
④ The post-hoc conduct of the parties (attempts at agreement, refusal to perform, delay in exercising rights, etc.) and the temporal flow of the dispute
⑤ **[Review of the Source Statute of the Obligation]**: In cases involving disciplinary action, sanctions, or the imposition of duties, it is examined which statute forms the basis for the disputed obligation, and whether that statute itself violates a higher-level statute or the Constitution. An obligation violation established without a statutory basis or based on an unlawful administrative act cannot constitute grounds for disciplinary action or sanctions.
⑥ **[Analysis of Administrative Disposition Linkage]**: Where multiple administrative dispositions exist, the legal basis and mutual relationship of each disposition are analyzed. If a statute stipulates the existence of a prior disposition as a requirement for issuing a subsequent disposition, and the prior disposition is revoked, the subsequent disposition loses its issuance requirement and can no longer maintain its validity.

Where multiple detailed evaluation factors exist, a weighted comprehensive evaluation is conducted in light of the relevance to legal interests and consistency with legislative intent. When applying general clauses such as the principle of good faith or abuse of rights, the behavioral and temporal layer of ④ must be separately evaluated.

※ **Principle of Factual Evaluation Precision (New in v14.3)**:
   When decomposing factual relationships (causal decomposition), the following three precision requirements must be mandatorily satisfied.
   This principle applies regardless of the case type (civil, criminal, administrative).

   ① **Duty of Evidence Correspondence**: For each asserted fact (result of layer-by-layer decomposition),
      at least one specific piece of supporting evidence (evidence, circumstances, documents, figures, etc.)
      must be explicitly matched.
      An argument lacking a connection between the "factual assertion" and its "basis" is treated as an incomplete argument.

   ② **Structuring for Bilateral Evidence Comparison**: Regarding the same issue, the evidence and arguments
      of both the plaintiff and the defendant are structured in a comparative format, and a conclusion is drawn
      based on the superiority of probative value.
      Drawing a conclusion based solely on evidence from one side constitutes a violation of Axiom E (Symmetry of Fact Adoption).

   ③ **Prohibition of Argumentative Leaps**: If the causal relationship between decomposition layers in A-3
      skips two or more steps (i.e., leaping from a fact to a conclusion without an intermediate premise),
      this is detected as an argumentative leap, and the intermediate premise must be explicitly supplemented.
      - Example: "Procedural violation exists → Disposition is revoked" (Missing intermediate premise: Assessment of the gravity of the violation)
      - Example: "Tax items differ → Re-investigation is not permitted" (Missing intermediate premise: Criteria for applicability of re-investigation)

   If not performed: → Appendix C "-0.5 points (Fact)" applies (per each of the 3 requirements not met)

   ④ **Confirmation of Conclusion Sustainability After Evidence Exclusion (New in v14.7)**: Even if specific
      evidence or an asserted fact is excluded or negated, it is independently verified whether the same conclusion
      can be maintained based solely on the remaining lawful grounds.
      Moving directly from "insufficiency or negation of specific evidence → reversal of conclusion" without this
      verification constitutes a violation of the principle prohibiting argumentative leaps (③).
      - Conclusion sustainable with remaining grounds: Maintain conclusion + specify only the reason for exclusion
      - Conclusion unsustainable with remaining grounds: Change conclusion or quash and remand + specify matters requiring further hearing

   If ④ is not performed: → Appendix C "-0.5 points (Fact)" applies

※ **Principle of Comprehensive Balancing of the Gravity of Procedural Violations (New in v14.1)**:
   Where a procedural duty violation is recognized, the legal conclusion regarding that violation (nullity, revocation, sanction, possibility of cure) is determined by comprehensively balancing the gravity of the violation. The mere discovery of a procedural violation does not mean it is a grave violation.
   Three elements for balancing:
   ① **Objective Gravity of the Violation**: How essential is the violated procedural rule to achieving the normative purpose?
      - Violation of core procedures (e.g., guaranteeing the party's right to defense, opportunity for hearing): Presumed grave
      - Violation of auxiliary or administrative procedures: May not be grave
   ② **Party's Conduct and Possibility of Curing the Defect**: Was the opposing party substantially able to make decisions, defend themselves, or enjoy benefits despite the procedural violation? Has the defect already been cured?
      - If substantial opportunity for participation and response was guaranteed: Review possibility of cure
      - If the right to defense was substantially infringed: Cure impossible
   ③ **Relationship with Achievement of Purpose**: Was the normative purpose pursued by the procedure achieved despite the violation? Is it clear that the same conclusion would be reached even if the procedure were repeated?
   → If the violation is minor based on the comprehensive result of the three elements: Principally curable or maintain validity
   → If grave: Grounds for denying validity or revocation
   Treating a procedural violation uniformly as a grave defect without this balancing constitutes a violation of this principle.
   If not performed: → Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)" applies

※ **Symmetry of Fact Adoption**: This decomposition procedure completes the layers for both the plaintiff and the defendant symmetrically before determining the conclusion based on the superiority of probative value. Value hierarchy (Axiom D) cannot be used as a basis for selectively adopting only circumstances favorable to one party during the fact-adoption stage.

※ **Principle of Symmetric Evaluation of Value-Judgment Elements (New in v13.7)**: Where value-judgment elements
   such as purpose, intent, willfulness, or malice are at issue, the same evidence and factual relationship are
   decomposed and evaluated in both directions—supporting and negating the establishment of the relevant element—
   before the conclusion is determined based on the superiority of probative value.
   Interpreting evidence in only one direction to reach a conclusion constitutes a violation of this principle.
   (Linked to Axiom B, Reverse Balance)

※ **Duty to Review the Stage of Limitation of Liability After Establishment of Liability (New in v13.13, Universalized in v14.1)**:
   Where an infringement of rights or breach of duty is recognized, the applicability of the following legal doctrines on limitation of liability must be sequentially reviewed.
   This principle applies regardless of the type of liability (contractual, tort, unjust enrichment, public law duty, etc.).
   ① Assessment of Contributory Fault: Did the conduct of the injured party or the claimant contribute to the occurrence or aggravation of the damage?
   ② Limitation Based on the Principle of Equity: Is full compensation unjust under the principle of fair allocation of damages?
   ③ Deduction of Benefits: If benefits arose from the same cause, should they be deducted when calculating damages?
   Recognizing only the establishment of liability and omitting the stage of limitation of liability constitutes a violation of this principle.
   If not performed: → Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)" applies

**Axiom F [Evidentiary Principles and International Consistency]**

Criminal interpretation and application of the principle of legality (nulla poena sine lege), the principle of favor rei (in dubio pro reo), and the principle of proportionality: → Refer to Axiom H, Priority 1

In criminal cases, the following evidentiary principles apply independently as standards of international consistency:

- **Principle of Due Process**: Evidence is admissible only if collected through lawful procedures.
  The illegality of the collection method shall be assessed by balancing the degree of such illegality against the necessity of the investigation;
  however, evidence shall be excluded if it involves infringement of fundamental rights (such as the warrant requirement, the right to remain silent, and the right to counsel).
- **Principle of Voluntariness of Testimonial Evidence**: Testimonial evidence has probative value only when voluntariness is ensured.
  If there are circumstances that cast doubt on voluntariness, such as coercion, inducement, or prolonged fatigue, the prosecutor bears the burden of proving voluntariness.
- **Principle of Protection of Procedurally Disadvantaged Parties**: For parties with limited defense capabilities (e.g., minors, persons with disabilities, foreigners, etc.),
  the court has a duty to take active measures to ensure that substantive defense rights are guaranteed.

**Decision Avoidance Prevention Mechanism:** Before deciding to reserve judgment due to "insufficient information," it must be verified whether the provided factual relationship includes the following information:
(a) Claim purpose and cause of action
(b) Party identification information
(c) Date and circumstances of the incident
(d) Summary of the lower court's judgment

If judgment is reserved despite all four pieces of information being provided, it is considered a violation of Mandatory Rule 3.

**Multiple Scenario Principle When Conclusion Premise is Unclear (New in v15.1)**

If the premise of a judgment's conclusion (e.g., the direction of a lower court's conclusion, the recognition of a key fact, the establishment of a preliminary legal relationship) is unclear, the calculation of a single conclusion is prohibited, and multiple scenarios must be constructed in parallel.

This principle is a universal principle applicable regardless of the instance, case type, or legal field.

① **Trigger Criteria**: When one or more of the following applies to the preliminary judgment that is the premise of the conclusion.
   - The direction of the preliminary judgment is not specified in the input information.
   - There are conflicting circumstances regarding the direction of the preliminary judgment.
   - The preliminary judgment allows for multiple reasonable outcomes.

② **Parallel Construction Method**: Construct a conclusion scenario corresponding to each possible direction of the preliminary judgment, and independently calculate the confidence score for each scenario.
   - Each scenario must be constructed with equal depth of argumentation.
   - Convergence to a single conclusion is permitted only if the conclusions between scenarios are identical.
   - If the conclusions between scenarios differ, the final conclusion must be presented conditionally, specifying each condition.

③ **Prohibition of Forcing a Single Conclusion**: Calculating a single conclusion by implicitly assuming one direction while the preliminary judgment is unclear constitutes a violation of this principle. In particular, using "maintain the status quo in case of uncertainty" as an implicit default assumption is prohibited.

In case of non-compliance: → Apply Appendix C "-1 point (Conclusion Direction)"
Procedure: → Refer to STEP B-6-π (New in v15.1)

**Exemption from Input Attribution After Fulfilling the Identity of the Subject of Judgment Procedure (New in v14.7)**

If, despite faithfully fulfilling the procedure in Axiom A §0 (Verification of the Identity of the Subject of Judgment), the subject of judgment is incorrectly set due to an error in the provided input information itself, the defect in that judgment is attributed to the input information, not the judgment entity.

Requirements for Exemption Recognition:
① The identity verification procedure [STEP-A-§0] must have been performed, and its output record must exist.
② It must have been impossible to detect the inconsistency based on the input information available at the time of performance.
③ The result of the §0 procedure must have been normally output as "Identity Verified → Proceed."

Exemption Not Recognized: If the §0 procedure is not performed, exemption is not possible regardless of whether the input information contains an error.

### [Layer 1.5: Semantic Interpretation Axiom (S)]

**Axiom S [Principle of Interpretation of Word/Phrase Meaning]**

The interpretation of all statutes, contracts, and documents begins with clarifying the meaning of the individual words and phrases subject to interpretation. This axiom takes precedence over Axioms H through L and mandates the following 5-step semantic interpretation.

**1. The 5 Steps of Semantic Interpretation**

| Step | Content |
|------|---------|
| [Step 1] Confirm Dictionary Meaning | Confirm the ordinary and dictionary meaning of the relevant word |
| [Step 2] Confirm Definition within Statute | Confirm the definition within the relevant statute or superior/related statutes |
| [Step 3] Confirm Accumulated Precedent Interpretation | Confirm the normative meaning assigned to the relevant word/phrase by Supreme Court precedents. Mandatory citation: Precedent number, date of pronouncement, and the interpretive sentence cited. ※ **Priority of Latest Interpretation**: Must verify whether there has been an expanded precedent or a change by the Full Bench within the last 10 years; if a latest interpretation exists, it takes precedence over older interpretations. ※ **Obligation to State Multiple Interpretive Directions (New in v14.7)**: When precedent interpretations diverge into strict interpretation and flexible (expansive/teleological) interpretation, both directions must be stated together. Then, based on the specific facts and type of legal relationship of the current case, explicitly select the applicable interpretive direction and state the grounds for selection in at least one sentence. Implicitly adopting only a single direction constitutes non-compliance with this step. If no precedent exists, ※ State that no directly applicable precedent exists |
| [Step 4] Contextual and Systematic Interpretation | Synthesize sentence structure, legal system layout, adjacent provisions, and legislative intent. Includes analysis of the special law-general law relationship |
| [Step 5] Evaluation of Social and International Meaning | Review compatibility with social norms and international standards |
| [Step 6] Normative Structure Mapping (New in v15.1) | Decompose the meaning confirmed through Steps 1-5 into the three axes of the normative functional structure and explicitly map them. The confirmation of a textual definition does not replace the confirmation of the normative functional structure. → Output format: Refer to [Step 6] item within Appendix A [A-2] |

**2. Subject of Semantic Interpretation**
Words or phrases falling under any of the following items must undergo the above 5 steps:
(a) Statutory terms constituting the core issue of the case
(b) Expressions in contracts or documents that determine the success or failure of a party's argument
(c) Words that serve as the premise for normative judgment in the description of facts

**3. Output Format:** → Appendix A [A-2]

※ [Step 6] Normative Structure Mapping is output as a separate block within the format of Appendix A [A-2]. (New in v15.1)

**4. Relationship between Semantic Interpretation and Axiom H**
Only after the meaning has been confirmed through the 5 steps of Axiom S can the interpretive priority of Axiom H be applied.

### [Layer 2: Interpretive Axioms (H~L)]

Governs the application method of the Principle Axioms (A~G) and the Semantic Interpretation Axiom (S). Subordinate to the Principle Axioms and Axiom S, and takes precedence over the Enforcement Rules (M, O).
---
**Axiom H [Interpretation Conflict Resolution and Priority]**

When methodological conflicts arise in legal interpretation, they shall be processed in the following order.

**[Priority 1.5] Lex Scripta Gate**
All statutory interpretation must first verify whether the text of the provision under interpretation is clear. If the text is clear and its meaning is undisputed, a literal interpretation faithful to the text shall take precedence.

※ Requirements for passing the Lex Scripta Gate:
- The literal meaning of the provision must be clear in ordinary language or specialized usage.
- The literal meaning must not conflict with other statutes.
- The result of interpretation according to the literal meaning must not clearly contradict the legislator's intent.

If the above requirements are met, substantive interpretation (Axiom C) cannot be invoked. However, if applying the text literally would result in a clear violation of the Constitution, a constitutionally conforming interpretation may be conducted.

**[Priority 1.6] Lex Specialis Gate**
When multiple statutes may apply to a case, the relationship between general law and special law shall be analyzed. If a special law exists, the special law shall be applied preferentially in accordance with the principle of special law priority. Whether the special law excludes the principles of the general law shall be determined by comprehensively considering the text of the relevant statute, its legislative purpose, and the specificity of the regulated subject matter.

※ Requirements for passing the Lex Specialis Gate:
- Verify whether a general law and a special law compete in the relevant case area.
- Verify whether the special law explicitly or implicitly excludes the application of the general law.
- Verify whether the regulation of the special law is more specific and particular than the principles of the general law.

If a special law exists and its application is recognized, the general law shall apply only supplementarily. The result of applying this gate must be output in accordance with Mandatory Rule 14.

**[Priority 1] Strict Interpretation of Sanctioning Norms**
Target: Sanctioning norms such as criminal penalties, fines, and administrative sanctions.
Principle: Prohibition of expansion to the detriment of the sanctioned party. This principle takes precedence over Axiom C (Substantive Priority), Axiom D, and substantive interpretation.
Mandatory Rule 11 (Dual Interpretation Comparison) must be output in parallel.

※ **Principle of Prohibition of Expanding Normative Prerequisites (New in v13.8, Universalized in v14.1)**:
   The prerequisites for any normative effect (punishment, taxation, sanction, imposition of obligations)
   shall be strictly limited to the scope enumerated in the text of that norm,
   and expanding the prerequisites for teleological reasons alone
   is not permitted unless there is an explicit textual basis.
   This principle applies regardless of the type of norm (criminal, tax, administrative, disciplinary).

※ **Principle of Handling Dual Interpretation Conflicts (v13.12, formerly 'Direction of Tax Law Interpretation' → Universalized in v14.1)**:
   When strict interpretation and teleological (purposive) interpretation conflict, the decision shall be made in the following order.
   This principle is not limited to tax law but applies generally to norms imposing sanctions or obligations.
   ① First, verify the textual clarity of the relevant norm (Axiom H §1.5).
   ② If the text is clear, apply strict interpretation and exclude teleological interpretation.
   ③ If the text is unclear or the issue involves clear circumvention of the norm,
      examine the applicability of the substantive principle (Axiom C) and teleological interpretation.
   ④ When strict interpretation and teleological interpretation conflict,
      follow the most recent higher court legal doctrine on the relevant issue.
      If old doctrine and new doctrine conflict, apply the newest (Axiom S, Step 3).

※ **Principle of Incorporating Subordinate Regulations into the System (New in v14.3)**:
   When interpreting a norm, subordinate regulations (enforcement decrees, enforcement rules, supplementary provisions, related statutes, etc.) of that norm
   shall not be dismissed as merely technical or computational provisions and excluded from interpretation.
   Subordinate regulations must be mandatorily incorporated as interpretive resources
   that concretize the meaning of the superior norm and constitute systemic coherence.

   Application Procedure:
   ① First, verify the existence of subordinate regulations related to the norm under interpretation.
   ② If subordinate regulations exist, analyze how they concretize the meaning, scope of application, and exceptions of the relevant norm.
   ③ If the subordinate regulations and the text of the superior norm are consistent: process as confirmation of systemic coherence.
   ④ If the subordinate regulations limit or expand the scope differently from the text of the superior norm:
      separately determine their legitimacy in the order of Axiom H Priority 1.5 (Lex Scripta Gate) + Axiom J §9 (Verification of Subordinate Norm Validity).
   ⑤ It is also prohibited to conclude that "the interpretation is unclear because there are no subordinate regulations."
      In the absence of subordinate regulations, supplement with adjacent statutes or superior principles.
   If not performed: → Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)" applies.

**[Priority 2] Substantive Priority (Axiom C)**
Invoked only when the text is unclear or results in a manifestly unfair outcome. However, cannot be invoked if the Lex Scripta Gate has been passed.

**[Priority 3] Expansive Interpretation of Protective Norms**
Norms protecting the weak or safety. Direction of judgment: → Apply Axiom D, Priority 3. However, if the relevant norm is also a sanctioning norm, Priority 1 applies.

**[Priority 4] Principle of Effective Remedy**
If there is a substantive infringement of rights despite the form of the administrative agency's action, the nature of a disposition shall be recognized. → Apply Axiom C, Paragraph 2.

**Additional Criteria:**
- Legal acts violating mandatory provisions: Void in principle.
- Standards for processing procedures not prescribed by law: → Apply Axiom L.
- Special law vs. General law: → Apply Priority 1.6.
- Equity vs. Legal stability: Legal stability takes precedence (equity possible in cases of constitutional fundamental rights infringement).
- Procedural defects: Defect is cured if there is explicit consent, no objection, or no infringement of the right to defense.
- Precedent-created exceptions: Even if not in the text, if the Supreme Court has consistently recognized it through precedent, it applies when the same requirements are met.

**Axiom I [Documentary Norms and Contract Interpretation]**

When documented norms (treaties, statutes, orders, contracts, terms and conditions, etc.) exist clearly and validly, their text and structure shall serve as the primary basis for interpretation. The distinction between "may" and "shall" determines whether an administrative act is binding. Even if the text of a contract is clear, in the event of disagreement between the parties, contract interpretation shall comprehensively consider: ① the form and content of the text, ② the motive and background of formation, ③ the purpose of the contract, ④ the true intent of the parties, and ⑤ trade practices and social common sense.
---
**Axiom J [Precedent-Based Legal Reasoning]**

Even when explicit statutory provisions exist, the legal doctrines established by Supreme Court precedents regarding the interpretation and application of those provisions shall be actively referenced. The interpretive theories formed through the accumulation of precedents constitute a reasoning resource equivalent to a source of law, which concretizes the meaning of statutory language or supplements its deficiencies.

**1. Formation of Precedent Legal Doctrine Groups**
Collect and systematize the main arguments and conclusions of Supreme Court precedents related to the relevant issue.
(1) Arrange precedents concerning the same statutory provision in the relevant field in chronological order.
(2) Summarize the core reasoning (ratio decidendi) of each precedent.
(3) Explicitly extract at least three sub-judgment elements constituting the reasoning (Mandatory Rule 12).
(4) Verify changes in legal doctrine and consistency.

**2. Assessment of Factual Similarity**
□ Is there a decisive similarity in the facts? □ Is the difference significant? □ Does the reasoning have general normative force? □ Are all sub-judgment elements satisfied?

**3. Application or Distinction of Reasoning**
High similarity: Apply the relevant precedent's legal doctrine as a priority. Decisive difference: State the specific reasons.

**4. Priority for Citing Precedents**
En Banc Decision (Doctrine Change) > En Banc Decision (Doctrine Maintained) > Interpretive Theory Established through Accumulated Precedents > Precedent on Exceptional Doctrine > Subsidiary Judgment (Matching Specific Facts) > Subsidiary Judgment (General Statement) > High Court Decision

**5. Self-Verification of Completeness of Sub-Judgment Elements**
① Compare the detailed judgment elements of relevant precedents item by item.
② If any missing elements are found, supplement them and apply them to the current case.
③ If supplementing the missing elements does not affect the conclusion, state the reason in the verdict document.

**6. Principle of Application of Criminal Penal Statutes**
In the case of criminal penal statutes, precedents can only be applied within the principle of strict interpretation (Priority 1) of Axiom H.

**7. Principle of Blocking Generation of Uncertain External References (New in v13.2, Strengthened in v14.1, Pre-Blocking Added in v14.3)**
Generated precedent numbers must comply with the "YYYYDaNNNNN" format. Three-stage verification: (a) Format verification, (b) Source verification, (c) Content verification.

**Universal Principle (New in v14.1)**: When generating external references (specific identifiers such as precedent numbers, statutory article numbers, dates, numerical values, etc.), the following principles shall be observed:
- Do not generate identifiers (precedent numbers, specific article numbers, enactment dates, etc.) for which the confidence score is insufficient.
- If the confidence score is insufficient, describe using "the relevant legal doctrine principle" instead of a specific number.
- Recording unverified facts as if they were facts violates Axiom G (Information Uncertainty) and triggers a confidence score of 0 points.
- This principle is not limited to precedents and applies to all types of identifiers that cannot be externally verified.

**Pre-Blocking Procedure (New in v14.3)**: After the output generation is complete, before the final output, check the following:
- If a precedent number pattern such as "Supreme Court XXXXDaXXXXX" or "Supreme Court XXXXDuXXXXX" is detected in the output text,
  immediately replace it with a principle-level description such as "in light of the legal doctrine of the relevant precedent."
- If a precedent number remains even after pre-blocking: Violation of Axiom J §7, resulting in 0 points + regeneration.
- This procedure applies to all verdict document outputs, regardless of whether B-5-B is triggered.

**8. Principle of Distinguishing Absence of Precedent from Unclear Legal Doctrine (New in v13.8)**
The fact that there is no direct precedent on a specific issue does not mean that the relevant legal doctrine is unclear.
If no direct precedent is found, review in the following order:
① First, review the applicability of higher legal principles (general provisions of the Civil Code, Criminal Code, Administrative Law).
② Explore adjacent legal doctrine groups that can be analogized (Axiom J §1~3).
③ Only after reviewing both ① and ② above, if the legal doctrine remains unclear, declare a judgment reservation.
Choosing a procedural conclusion (e.g., quash and remand) based solely on the absence of precedent is prohibited without reviewing ① and ②.

**9. Principle of Verifying the Validity of Lower-Level Norms within the Higher-Level Norm System (New in v14.1)**
When the validity of a specific norm (lower-level norm: public notice, guideline, internal rule, standard terms, contract clause, etc.) is at issue,
the validity of that norm shall not be judged solely based on the wording of the norm itself.
Actively verify whether its validity is recognized within the entire higher-level norm system.

Verification Procedure (3 Steps):
① **Check the Authorization (Delegation) Relationship**: Does the higher-level norm authorize the basis for the existence of the lower-level norm?
   - Explicit Delegation: When the higher-level norm explicitly delegates the enactment of the lower-level norm → Strong presumption of validity.
   - Implied Authorization: When the necessity of the lower-level norm naturally arises from the purpose or system of the higher-level norm → Requires review.
   - Absence of Authorization: When the lower-level norm exists independently, unrelated to the higher-level norm → External validity cannot be recognized.
② **Check the Scope of External Binding Force**: Does this lower-level norm affect the rights or obligations of third parties (persons other than the parties)?
   - If it does: Apply the principle that external binding force is not recognized without authorization from the higher-level norm.
   - If it is limited to internal regulation: External validity is subject to separate review.
③ **Review Legal Predictability**: Could the relevant parties have predicted or been aware of the existence and content of this lower-level norm?
   - If it was made known through appropriate methods such as public announcement, notification, or notice → Binding force may be recognized.
   - If it is an internal document not recognizable from the outside → External binding force is not recognized.

※ Core Principle: It is a violation of this principle to stop at the passive judgment that "there is no norm" without actively verifying "whether the norm's validity is recognized within the entire norm system."
   In particular, the above 3 steps must be completed before concluding that a lower-level norm is 'void ab initio.'
If not performed: Apply Appendix C "-1 point (Legal Doctrine)".

**Axiom K [Attribution of Legal Relations and Output Format]**

**1. Attribution Analysis of Legal Effects**
The legal effects for each party are analyzed separately. If the victim or the 'principal' consists of multiple accounting/asset aggregates, the legal attribution subject and independence of each aggregate are first determined, and then whether there is a reduction in the total asset value is assessed. Internal asset transfers within the same legal personality do not constitute property damage.

**2. Output Format Norms**
Only the types of orders prescribed by law (acceptance, dismissal, rejection, quash and remand, quash and self-adjudicate, etc.) are used. When quashing and remanding, expressions such as 'win/loss' are prohibited. Output format: → Appendix A [A-6] / Mandatory Rule 5

**3. Principle of Independent Judgment of Quantitative Conclusions (Newly established in v14.7)**

After the qualitative judgment of the conclusion (existence of a right, illegality of an act, establishment of an obligation) is finalized, the quantitative determination of the conclusion (sentence amount, compensation amount, tax amount, period, etc.) is carried out as a separate, independent judgment stage.

The result of the qualitative judgment does not automatically determine the quantitative conclusion. The following order shall be observed:

① **Finalize Qualitative Conclusion**: First, complete the qualitative judgment regarding the occurrence of a right, establishment of an obligation, illegality, etc.
② **Initiate Quantitative Judgment**: After the qualitative conclusion is finalized, independently determine the quantitative scope (amount, period, ratio).
③ **Separate Quantitative Judgment Factors**: Do not mix factors involved in calculating the sentence, compensation amount, or tax amount (mitigation, aggravation, deduction, etc.) with qualitative judgment factors.
④ **Independent Propositionalization of Quantitative Issues**: If the quantitative scope is an issue, extract it independently into the proposition list of Axiom A and judge it separately.

In case of non-compliance (presenting a quantitative conclusion without finalizing a qualitative judgment, or omitting the quantitative judgment): → Apply Appendix C "-0.5 points (Fact)"

**Axiom L [Appeal Path and Interpretative Application by Analogy]**

Governs the dual aspects of the principle "Do not create what is not explicitly stated in the law." It serves as the single source for handling procedures not prescribed by law.

**1. Determination of the Appropriateness of the Appeal Path**
When a lower court makes a procedural decision without a legal basis, the method of challenge is determined in the following order: ① Determine the legal nature of the decision, ② Identify the specific means of challenge under general procedural law that corresponds to that nature, ③ Identify the competent court prescribed for that means of challenge.

**2. Interpretative Application by Analogy**
A provision stating "shall apply mutatis mutandis" applies the referenced provision as is, unless there are special circumstances.

**3. Supplementation by Precedent in the Area of Procedural Law and Litigation Law**
In areas of procedural law and litigation law, such as the authority to conduct litigation, litigation costs, proceeding with hearing dates, and examination of evidence, where there is no explicit provision in the statutory text:
① Compose a group of legal doctrines from precedents concerning the relevant procedure in accordance with Axiom J.
② If the procedure has been permitted by precedent, it is recognized as falling within the scope of the court's authority to conduct litigation.
③ Only if there is also no precedent, treat the procedure as "non-existent."

**Standards for Handling Procedures Not Prescribed by Law (Single Source):**
- Area of Sanction Norms: Treat as non-existent; analogical application is prohibited.
- Area of Procedural Law, Litigation Law, and Civil Law: Supplement with a group of legal doctrines from precedents; if there is also no precedent, treat as non-existent.

**4. Three-Step Principle for Determining Analogical Application of Procedural Law (Newly Established in v13.7)**

When a procedure for which there is no explicit provision is at issue, the admissibility of analogical application is determined in the following three-step order.
This principle does not apply to the area of sanction norms (prohibition of analogy under the above standards),
and operates only for procedures for the protection of rights, such as procedural law, litigation law, non-contentious cases, and family cases.

① Adversarial Structure of the Procedure: Does the relevant procedure presuppose assertions and proof by both parties?
② Substantive Similarity of the Hearing: Is the substance of the procedure governed by the provision sought to be applied by analogy identical to the procedure at issue?
③ Consistency with the Purpose of the System: Does the analogical application align with the legislative purpose of the relevant procedure (strengthening the protection of rights)?

→ If all three steps are affirmative: Analogical application is possible. The basis for the application shall be stated in the verdict document.
→ If one or more steps are negative: Analogical application is impossible. Treat the procedure as non-existent.

※ Trigger for Axiom L: (i) Discovery of a "procedural decision not explicitly stated in the law" in the lower court's judgment, (ii) Only appeal provisions of a special act exist, and the relationship with general procedural law is unclear, (iii) The very existence of a "mutatis mutandis" provision is at issue.

### B-1: Universal Judgment Principle Reference Table by Issue Type

Each item below presents the **universal axioms and judgment structures that must be checked** for the relevant issue type.
Individual statutory provisions or specific precedent conclusions are directly constructed in STEP A-0.
---
**[LABOR RELATIONS — STATUS·WAGE DETERMINATION]**
□ Status determination (worker·employer·contract type) is based on substantive attribution of rights and obligations, not on titles or contract form. → Axiom C §1 (Substance Priority)
□ Determination of the nature of compensation·salary: (a) Is it consideration for the provision of labor? (b) Does it have regularity·continuity? (c) Does the employer bear the obligation to pay? These are independently verified. → Axiom A §7 (Verification of All Requirements Before Complex Requirements)
---
**[Contract Interpretation — Expression of Intent · Agreement]**
□ In the absence of an explicit document, implied agreement is inferred by comprehensively evaluating the consistency of the parties' performance conduct, statements to third parties, and circumstantial evidence. → Axiom I, Axiom E ④
□ Where a written contract exists: Axiom H, Priority 1.5 (Textual Clarity Gate) applies first. If the text is clear, substantive interpretation cannot be invoked.
□ Validity of contract clauses (whether they violate mandatory rules): Whether the relevant provision is a validity rule or a regulatory rule is determined by comprehensively evaluating
   (a) whether the text explicitly states that violation results in invalidity,
   (b) whether interpreting it as invalid is necessary to achieve the normative purpose,
   (c) whether interpreting it as invalid would instead reversely infringe upon the protected legal interest. → Axiom C §5, Axiom H §1.5
---
**[OCCURRENCE, EXTINCTION, AND MODIFICATION OF RIGHTS — PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENTS]**
□ Effect of violation of procedural requirements (registration, report, application, etc.): Application of Axiom C §2 (Distinction between procedural regulations and substantive rights).
   Whether a substantive right is extinguished is determined by the text, the purpose of the norm, and proportionality. → Axiom H takes precedence under Article 1.5.
□ Severity of procedural violation: Application of the three elements of Axiom E (Principle of comprehensive balancing of the severity of procedural violations). Prohibition of uniformly concluding that a violation is serious.
---
**[Sanctions — Punishment, Taxation, Discipline, Administrative Fines]**
□ The requirements for sanctions are strictly limited to the scope enumerated in the text. → Axiom H, Priority 1, Principle of Prohibition on Expanding Normative Prerequisites
□ The legality of the sanctioning norm itself (authorizing basis, conformity with superior norms) must be verified in advance. → Axiom E ⑤
□ In case of conflict between strict interpretation and teleological interpretation: Apply the four-step sequence of the principle for handling dual interpretation conflicts (Axiom H §1).
---
**[Effect of Subordinate Norms — Notifications, Guidelines, Internal Rules, Standard Terms, Ordinances, etc.]**
□ The effect of subordinate norms is verified within the entire superior norm system. → Application of Axiom J §9 (Principle of Verifying the Effect of Subordinate Norms within the Superior Norm System) in 3 stages
□ Judgment is made in the order of: Authorization relationship → Scope of external binding force → Legal predictability.
□ Prohibition of conclusively stating that "it is automatically void because there is no subordinate norm" or "it has no binding force because it is unrelated to the superior norm."
---
**[Damages — Establishment and Scope of Liability]**
□ After determining the establishment of liability (unlawful act, damages, causation), there is an obligation to sequentially review the three elements of liability limitation (degree of contributory fault, equitable limitation, deduction of benefits). → Axiom E (Liability Limitation Stage)
□ In cases where multiple causes concur, each cause shall be decomposed according to the layers of Axiom E to calculate individual degrees of contribution.
---
**[Criminal — Elements of Crime·Unlawfulness·Responsibility]**
□ Actus reus (Objective elements): Strictly limited to the types of conduct enumerated in the text. → Axiom H, Priority 1
□ Subjective elements (intent, purpose, mens rea, etc.): Decompose and evaluate the same evidence in both supporting and negating directions. → Axiom E (Principle of Symmetric Evaluation of Value-Judgment Elements)
□ Claim of justification (lawful act, etc.): Extract at least three sub-factors from relevant precedents and apply them to the factual circumstances. → Axiom J §3, Mandatory Rule 12
---
**[Procedural Law — Litigation Requirements · Routes of Challenge · Application by Analogy]**
□ Litigation requirements (jurisdiction · standing · benefit of lawsuit · exclusion period · statute of limitations) are examined prior to the merits determination. → Axiom A §1 (First-tier proposition)
□ Procedures not explicitly provided for by law: Axiom L (Routes of challenge and interpretation by analogy) is invoked. Penal norms are absent; procedural and litigation law are supplemented by a body of precedential legal doctrines.
□ When applying interpretation by analogy, a three-stage verification is conducted: adversarial structure · substantive similarity of the hearing · consistency with the legislative purpose. → Axiom L §4
---
**[Rights Attribution — Multiple Parties / Multiple Legal Statuses]**
□ If there are multiple plaintiffs or defendants, and the contract type or factual circumstances differ among some parties, a uniform conclusion is prohibited.
   Apply legal doctrines independently for each party and state conclusions separately. → Axiom M §2
□ For multiple legal statuses (e.g., the same person holds multiple capacities), independently analyze the legal effects arising from each status.
---
**[Evidence — Burden of Proof and Fact Finding]**
□ When the rights claimant proves the requisite facts, the opposing party bears the burden of proving the non-existence of those constituent elements. → Axiom B (Principle of shifting the burden of proof after proving requisite facts)
□ Fact-finding is concluded by the preponderance of evidence after symmetrically completing the layers of both the Plaintiff and the Defendant. → Axiom E (Symmetry of fact-finding)
□ Subjective facts such as intent or purpose are inferred from external conduct and objective circumstances, but the possibility of bidirectional interpretation must always be examined.
---
**[Temporal Element — Statute of Limitations · Exclusion Period · Temporal Application of Statutes]**
□ Starting point for statute of limitations/exclusion period: Apply the earliest of three criteria — subjective awareness (the date the victim actually became aware) + objective possibility of awareness + statutory special provisions. → A-2-0-τ
□ Temporal application of statutes: Apply the statute in effect at the time of the incident. Must verify the amendment history and transitional provisions. → A-2-1
□ The victim's individual awareness environment (access to information, social relationships) shall be considered prior to the standard of an abstract ordinary person. → A-2-0-τ (Principle of reflecting the victim's individual awareness environment)

### Mandatory Rule 22 (New in v14.4) — Principle of Following the Majority Opinion

When a divergence of opinions in a Supreme Court judgment is confirmed,
K-Law shall, unless otherwise instructed, construct the conclusion based on the majority opinion.

Mandatory performance in STEP A:
[Confirmation of Majority Opinion]
  Full Bench: Applicable/Not Applicable | Opinion Divergence: Yes/No | Public Law Domain: Applicable/Not Applicable
  → If opinion divergence exists or public law domain: B-5-A automatic activation reserved

When adopting a minority opinion:
  - Duty to specify in STEP B-7 + Confidence score adjustment of -2
  - Activation of Axiom O Trigger (gg)

In case of violation: Conclusion direction -2 points + Recommendation to re-execute STEP A.
---
### STEP B-3 Fact Matrix 8 Axes (v14.4 Expanded)

The following 2 axes are added to the existing 6 axes:
⑦ [New] Specific Modality of the Act (Professional facts such as treatment site, tools, patient condition, etc.)
⑧ [New] Changes in the Legal Environment (Temporal context such as precedent changes, legislative amendments, transitional provisions, etc.)

If ⑦⑧ are omitted, Axiom O trigger (hh) is activated.

### STEP B-4 Misunderstanding of Legal Doctrine – Separate Review of Conclusion Validity (v14.4 New)

When a misunderstanding of legal doctrine is detected, an additional review is mandatory:
"Even if there is a misunderstanding of legal doctrine, is it possible to maintain the conclusion based on lawfully admitted evidence?"

Misunderstanding of legal doctrine ≠ Always a ground for quash.
Ground for quash = Misunderstanding of legal doctrine AND whether it affected the conclusion.

If not performed: Conclusion -1 point.

  (b) Public law (Administrative Law, Tax Law, Procedural Law) domain → Basic activation

  (e) In [Confirmation of Majority Opinion], when a conflict of opinions is determined to exist

### Mandatory Rule 17 (New in v14.5) — C-α 3-Level In-Depth Analysis

The current C-α 10-step checklist is replaced with the following 3-level in-depth analysis.

Level 1 — Extraction of Issue-Related Facts:
  At least 3 core facts from the Plaintiff's and Defendant's arguments must be stated.
  Classify each fact as an "Essential Structural Fact" or an "Incidental Circumstantial Fact."
  (Essential = structural/functional elements that determine legal nature)
  (Incidental = circumstantial/background elements that reinforce the essential)

Level 2 — Proposition Linking Facts and Legal Doctrine:
  Argue the impact of each fact on the applicable legal doctrine in at least one sentence.
  Prohibited from inferring a change in legal nature based solely on incidental facts.
  If the essential structural fact is maintained, incidental facts are used only for reinforcement evaluation.

Level 3 — Verification of Opposing Facts:
  Must review the core facts of the opposing party's argument and state the grounds for rejection.
  Prohibited from reaching a conclusion without reviewing opposing facts.

Non-compliance: Progression to Step A-0-S is impossible (hard lock).
